Our Story

Three generations,
one piece of land.

In 1987, Bill and Maree Greenfield planted their first 40 mango trees on a hillside block in the Sunshine Coast Hinterland. What started as a weekend project became a life's work — and eventually, a family legacy.

Today their grandchildren tend the same soil, now 180 acres of mixed stone fruit, citrus, and berries. We've never chased volume. We chase flavour, season by season, tree by tree.

We sell at our farm gate, at two local markets, and through a small home-delivery round. That's deliberately small — small enough that we know our soil, our trees, and often, our customers.

Albion Strawberries

Sun-kissed from dawn to your door. Picked before breakfast, on your table by lunch.

$6.50 / punnet

The Story

Grown in rows,
ripened in sun.

Our Albion strawberries grow in raised rows on the north-facing slope of the farm, where morning sun hits earliest and lingers longest. We've trialled a dozen varieties over the years. The Albion stayed — not because it's the easiest to grow, but because nothing else comes close for depth of flavour.

The Albion is a day-neutral variety, which means it produces fruit continuously rather than all at once. Our pickers walk the rows every morning at first light, selecting only berries that have fully coloured and yield just slightly under thumb pressure. If it's not ready, it stays on the plant.

Every punnet is field-cooled within 30 minutes of picking. No cold storage. No ethylene ripening. Just the real thing.

From Field to Punnet

How we grow ours

Three steps. No shortcuts. The way strawberries were always meant to be grown.

Raised Bed Planting

Each season we re-mound our beds with composted organic matter and certified coir fibre. No synthetic fertilisers — just what the soil genuinely needs.

Dawn Harvest

Picked before 6am while temperatures are still cool. Berries harvested in heat lose sweetness and firmness rapidly — ours never do.

Field-Cooled Fast

Within 30 minutes of picking, punnets move into our forced-air cooling shed. This locks in sugar, texture, and that bright berry fragrance you can smell before you open the lid.

Kensington Pride Mangoes

Tree-ripened. Never rushed. The Bowen mango the way Queensland intended it.

$14.00 / tray of 6

The Story

Thirty-year roots,
floral honey sweetness.

The Kensington Pride — Bowen mango to most Queenslanders — is the benchmark by which every other variety is judged. Ours come from trees planted in 1994, now over 30 years old, with root systems that reach deep into the volcanic basalt subsoil of the Sunshine Coast Hinterland.

Old trees produce less fruit but dramatically better fruit. The sugars concentrate. The fibres disappear. The fragrance becomes something entirely different from a young-tree mango — more floral, more complex, more unmistakably Queensland.

We test brix (sugar) levels before picking begins each day. If the reading isn't right, we wait. We'd rather sell fewer mangoes than disappoint a single customer.

From Blossom to Tray

Nine months of patience

A Kensington Pride mango takes from August blossom to November harvest. We don't cut corners at any stage.

Tree Management

Our trees are pruned annually to open the canopy, maximising airflow and sun penetration to every cluster. Healthier trees, better fruit. Simple.

Brix Testing

Every morning before picking, we test fruit from multiple trees with a refractometer. We don't start harvesting until brix hits our minimum threshold. No exceptions.

Hand Graded & Packed

Every mango is graded by hand for size, skin colour and any blemishes before being nestled in tissue-lined trays. Six per tray. Not seven, not five.

Organic Blueberries

Small. Dense. Grown slow under bird netting on our south paddock — never touched by pesticide.

$8.00 / 200g

The Story

Certified organic,
genuinely flavourful.

Most blueberries on supermarket shelves are grown for size and shelf life — not taste. Our O'Neal variety is the opposite: smaller, denser, with a flavour concentration that makes commercial blueberries taste like flavoured water in comparison.

We grow under permanent bird netting on the south paddock, which reduces UV stress on the fruit and keeps the berry skins tender rather than tough. The netting also means zero pesticide spray, since bird pressure (our biggest threat) is handled physically rather than chemically.

Each 200g pack is hand-sorted on the grading table — every berry inspected, no soft ones, no stems. What you get is the premium pick.

Zero Pesticides, All Flavour

How we keep them pure

Growing certified organic blueberries takes more work. We think the result speaks for itself.

Acid Soil Preparation

Blueberries demand soil pH between 4.5 and 5.5. We adjust annually with certified organic sulphur and pine bark mulch — no synthetic acidifiers, ever.

Permanent Bird Netting

The netting stays up year-round, protecting fruit from birds and reducing UV load. Softer skins, better colour, zero spray required. A win on every front.

Hand-Sorted & Packed

Every berry goes across the grading table. We remove anything soft, shrivelled or undersized. You pay for 200g of premium fruit — that's exactly what you receive.

Seedless Watermelon

Grown slow through the Queensland summer. Cut when they're genuinely ready — not a day before.

$12.00 / whole melon

The Story

Thumped for ripeness,
never rushed.

You can tell a Greenfield watermelon is ready the same way farmers have always known — the field tendril nearest the fruit dries and curls back completely. That's nature's signal, and it hasn't been wrong in 38 years of farming this land.

We grow our Crimson Seedless variety in long rows on the flat river paddock, which floods naturally in winter and leaves behind an extraordinary depth of topsoil. No irrigation tricks needed — by summer the soil holds enough moisture to grow enormous, deeply flavoured fruit.

Average weight at harvest is 8–10kg. We don't harvest smaller. A small watermelon is an unripe watermelon.

Field to Farm Gate

Summer's most satisfying harvest

Watermelons can't be rushed. Three months in the ground, harvested at a single perfect moment.

River Paddock Soil

The flat paddock along our creek floods each winter, depositing fresh alluvial silt. By spring, the soil is black and deep — exactly what watermelons need to develop real sweetness.

Field-Ripened Only

We wait for the tendril curl. We check the ground spot — it should be a creamy yellow, not white. We thump and listen for the deep, hollow resonance. Only then do we cut.

Same-Day Collection

Cut melons leave the paddock within hours. Unlike commercial operations, we don't hold stock in a shed for days. What we cut today is available today.

Valencia Oranges

The best juice you'll ever press. Thin-skinned, late-season, bursting with liquid gold.

$9.00 / 2kg bag

The Story

The juicer's orange,
done properly.

Valencia oranges are the world's most planted variety for a reason: the juice-to-peel ratio is extraordinary. Our trees sit on a north-east facing hillside block that gets consistent morning sun, which drives sugar development without the acid-stripping heat of a western exposure.

The Valencia is a late-season variety — our harvest window runs August through November, long after navels are finished. By August, the sugar has been building for months and the skin is paper-thin. Juice yield per kilogram is typically 40% higher than a supermarket navel orange bought the same week.

We bag them the morning of delivery. If you order by 10am, you'll be pressing by 3pm.

From Grove to Glass

Juice-grade quality,
every single bag

A Valencia grown well is a fundamentally different product. Here's why ours are different.

Grove Maintenance

Our citrus grove is hand-pruned each year to remove dead wood and improve airflow. Healthier trees produce thinner-skinned, juicier fruit — it's that direct a connection.

Organic Nutrition Program

We feed our citrus trees with a certified organic program of fish emulsion, compost tea and trace mineral sprays. No synthetic NPK. Slower, but the flavour difference is measurable.

Picked to Order

Unlike supermarket citrus that may sit in cold storage for weeks, our bags are picked the morning you order and delivered the same afternoon. Juice within 24 hours of leaving the tree.
